Legislation ​

Informational content only. This page organizes public and official references. It does not replace individual medical, legal or regulatory guidance.

Main regulatory timeline ​

Federal law

Law 11.343/2006

Brazil's drug law remains the core legal backdrop. It allows the Union, under inspection, to authorize cultivation for medicinal or scientific purposes.

STJ · Nov 13, 2024

IAC 16

Brazil's Superior Court of Justice recognized the legal possibility of cultivation by legal entities for medicinal and pharmaceutical purposes, with THC below 0.3%, and ordered regulation.

STJ · Feb 28, 2025

Deadline kept in place

The court denied extra time requested by Anvisa and the Union, keeping pressure on the regulatory timeline.

Anvisa · 2026

RDC 1.012 to 1.015/2026

Anvisa approved updated rules for research, production, patient associations and the manufacturing/import framework.

Why this matters ​

  • Exceptional import authorization remains a real path for individual patients.
  • Access through pharmacies and drugstores stays tied to Anvisa's sanitary framework.
  • Patient associations now sit inside a more explicit regulatory environment.
  • The ABPCM portal turns scattered official references into one readable path.
